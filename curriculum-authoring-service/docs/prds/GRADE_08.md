**Critical transition year.** Grade 8 introduces functions, linear equations, and the Pythagorean theorem. This is often the year that determines high school math placement (Algebra I vs. Geometry track). Content must be rigorous enough to prepare students for high school coursework.

## Subjects and Units

### Mathematics (Common Core 8.NS, 8.EE, 8.F, 8.G, 8.SP)

**NS003: The Number System**
- Know that numbers that are not rational are irrational (8.NS.A.1)
- Approximate irrational numbers and locate them on a number line (8.NS.A.2)
- Convert repeating decimals to rational number form (8.NS.A.1)

**EE003: Expressions and Equations**
- Know and apply the properties of integer exponents (8.EE.A.1)
- Use square root and cube root symbols; evaluate roots of small perfect squares/cubes (8.EE.A.2)
- Use numbers expressed in scientific notation (8.EE.A.3, 8.EE.A.4)
- Solve linear equations in one variable (8.EE.C.7)
- Analyze and solve pairs of simultaneous linear equations (8.EE.C.8)
- Understand the connections between proportional relationships, lines, and linear equations (8.EE.B.5, 8.EE.B.6)

**FUNC001: Functions**
- Understand that a function is a rule that assigns to each input exactly one output (8.F.A.1)
- Compare properties of two functions represented in different ways (8.F.A.2)
- Interpret the equation y = mx + b as defining a linear function (8.F.A.3)
- Determine the rate of change and initial value of a function (8.F.B.4)
- Describe qualitatively the functional relationship between two quantities by analyzing a graph (8.F.B.5)

**GEOM008: Geometry**
- Verify experimentally the properties of rotations, reflections, and translations (8.G.A.1)
- Understand congruence using rigid motions (8.G.A.2)
- Describe the effect of dilations and similarity (8.G.A.3, 8.G.A.4)
- Use informal arguments about angles formed by parallel lines cut by a transversal (8.G.A.5)
- Explain a proof of the Pythagorean theorem and its converse (8.G.B.6)
- Apply the Pythagorean theorem in real-world and mathematical problems (8.G.B.7, 8.G.B.8)
- Know the formulas for volumes of cylinders, cones, and spheres (8.G.C.9)

**SP003: Statistics and Probability**
- Construct and interpret scatter plots for bivariate measurement data (8.SP.A.1)
- Use the equation of a linear model to solve problems in context of bivariate data (8.SP.A.2, 8.SP.A.3)
- Understand patterns of association in bivariate categorical data using two-way frequency tables (8.SP.A.4)

### ELA (Common Core RL.8, RI.8, W.8, SL.8, L.8)

**LA001: Reading Literature**
- Cite the textual evidence that most strongly supports an analysis (RL.8.1)
- Determine a theme or central idea and analyze its development, including relationship to characters/setting/plot (RL.8.2)
- Analyze how particular lines of dialogue or incidents in a story propel the action (RL.8.3)
- Analyze how a modern work of fiction draws on themes, patterns, or character types from myths, traditional stories, or religious works (RL.8.9)

**LA002: Reading Informational Text**
- Cite the textual evidence that most strongly supports an analysis (RI.8.1)
- Determine a central idea and analyze its development over the course of the text (RI.8.2)
- Analyze in detail the structure of a specific paragraph, including the role of particular sentences (RI.8.5)
- Determine an author's point of view or purpose and analyze how the author acknowledges and responds to conflicting evidence (RI.8.6)
- Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of using different mediums to present a topic (RI.8.7)
- Delineate and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text, assessing reasoning and evidence (RI.8.8)
- Analyze two or more texts that provide conflicting information on the same topic (RI.8.9)

**LA003: Writing**
- Write arguments with sustained complexity, supporting claims with logical reasoning and relevant evidence (W.8.1)
- Write informative/explanatory texts to examine complex ideas (W.8.2)
- Write narratives with well-structured event sequences, reflection, and effective pacing (W.8.3)
- Conduct short and sustained research projects (W.8.7, W.8.8, W.8.9)

**LA004: Speaking and Listening**
- Engage effectively in collaborative discussions, building on others' ideas (SL.8.1)
- Analyze the purpose of information presented in diverse media and formats and evaluate the motives behind its presentation (SL.8.2)
- Present claims and findings, emphasizing salient points in a focused, coherent manner with sound reasoning (SL.8.4)

**LA005: Language**
- Recognize and correct inappropriate shifts in verb voice and mood (L.8.1a, L.8.1b)
- Form and use verbs in the active and passive voice (L.8.1b)
- Form and use verbs in the conditional and subjunctive mood (L.8.1c)
- Recognize and use verbals: gerunds, participles, and infinitives (L.8.1a)

**LA006: Vocabulary**
- Use context clues to determine meanings of words with multiple meanings (L.8.4a)
- Use Greek and Latin roots and affixes, especially for STEM vocabulary (L.8.4b)
- Interpret figures of speech: verbal irony and puns (L.8.5a)
- Distinguish among connotations of words with similar denotations (L.8.5c)

### Science (NGSS MS-PS2, MS-PS3, MS-PS4, MS-LS3, MS-LS4, MS-ESS3, MS-ETS1)

**SCI001: Forces and Motion**
- Plan an investigation to provide evidence that the change in motion depends on net force and mass (MS-PS2-2)
- Apply Newton's third law to design a solution to a problem (MS-PS2-1)
- Construct and interpret force diagrams (MS-PS2-2)
- Analyze gravitational force and its dependence on mass and distance (MS-PS2-4)
- Apply concepts of momentum to predict changes in motion (MS-PS2-2)

**SCI002: Energy**
- Construct and interpret data about the relationship between kinetic energy and mass/speed (MS-PS3-1)
- Develop a model to describe potential energy stored in various configurations (MS-PS3-2)
- Design and evaluate a device that converts energy from one form to another (MS-PS3-3)
- Apply scientific principles to describe the relationship between thermal energy and particle motion (MS-PS3-4)
- Describe wave energy and its relationship to amplitude and wavelength (MS-PS4-1)

**SCI003: Waves and Electromagnetic Radiation**
- Use mathematical representations to describe wave properties (MS-PS4-1)
- Develop and use a model to describe reflection, absorption, and transmission of waves (MS-PS4-2)
- Integrate information to explain how digitized signals encode and transmit information (MS-PS4-3)

**SCI004: Heredity**
- Develop and use a model to describe the structure of DNA (MS-LS3-1)
- Develop and use a model to describe the relationship between genes and proteins (MS-LS3-1)
- Construct an explanation for how sexual vs asexual reproduction contributes to genetic variation (MS-LS3-2)
- Understand the role of mutations in genetic variation (MS-LS3-1)

**SCI005: Earth and Human Activity**
- Construct a scientific explanation of how Earth's natural resources are unevenly distributed (MS-ESS3-1)
- Analyze and interpret data on natural hazards and human impacts (MS-ESS3-2)
- Apply scientific principles to design monitoring methods for minimizing human impact (MS-ESS3-3)
- Construct an argument supported by evidence for how increases in human population affect Earth systems (MS-ESS3-4)
- Ask questions to clarify evidence of climate change factors (MS-ESS3-5)
- Design engineering solutions to environmental problems (MS-ETS1-1, MS-ETS1-2)

### Social Studies (C3 Framework: D2.Civ, D2.Eco, D2.Geo, D2.His)

**SS001: Civics**
- Constitutional law and the structure of American government (D2.Civ.1.6-8, D2.Civ.3.6-8)
- Landmark Supreme Court cases and their impact (D2.Civ.12.6-8)
- Civil rights movements and their legacy (D2.Civ.10.6-8)
- Political processes: elections, legislation, lobbying (D2.Civ.2.6-8)

**SS002: Economics**
- Macroeconomics basics: GDP, inflation, unemployment (D2.Eco.9.6-8)
- Fiscal and monetary policy (D2.Eco.10.6-8)
- Globalization and its effects on national economies (D2.Eco.6.6-8)

**SS003: Geography**
- Geopolitics and regional conflicts (D2.Geo.3.6-8)
- Human geography patterns: population density, settlement (D2.Geo.7.6-8)
- Regional analysis and geographic reasoning (D2.Geo.8.6-8)

**SS004: History**
- American Revolution: causes, events, consequences (D2.His.1.6-8)
- Early Republic through Civil War (D2.His.2.6-8)
- Reconstruction and its legacy (D2.His.3.6-8)
- Industrialization and immigration (D2.His.14.6-8)

## Difficulty Calibration

- Starting difficulty: 3-5
- Ending difficulty: 6-9
- Target range for most content: 5-8
- Grade 8 is a transition year; difficulty should push higher than Grade 7 to prepare for high school rigor. Function and linear equation content should reach difficulty 8-9 at the top end.

## Graph Requirements

- Target edge density: 3.0-4.0 prerequisite edges per skill node (higher than Grade 7 due to increased interconnection)
- Key prerequisite chains:
  - Proportional relationships (from G7 RP002) -> Linear equations (EE003) -> Functions (FUNC001) -> Graph interpretation (critical path to Algebra I)
  - Integer exponents (EE003) -> Scientific notation -> Real-world applications
  - Rigid transformations (GEOM008) -> Congruence -> Similarity -> Pythagorean theorem (geometry chain)
  - Scatter plots (SP003) -> Lines of best fit -> Bivariate analysis (statistics chain)
  - DNA structure (SCI004) -> Genes and proteins -> Genetic variation -> Natural selection connection
  - American Revolution (SS004) -> Early Republic -> Civil War -> Reconstruction (chronological chain)
- Cross-subject edges: linear equations in science (force calculations), data analysis in social studies, scientific notation in science contexts

## Authoring Notes

- This is the single most consequential math year in the K-8 sequence. Students who master functions and linear equations here are prepared for Algebra I; those who do not may be placed in remedial tracks. Ensure extensive practice and multiple representations (tables, graphs, equations, verbal descriptions) for FUNC001.
- The Pythagorean theorem unit (GEOM008) should build from experimental verification through formal proof to application, not skip directly to formula application.
- Systems of equations (EE003) should include graphical, substitution, and elimination methods with clear connections between them.
- Science transitions from life science emphasis (Grade 7) to physical science emphasis (Grade 8). Forces and motion content should use mathematical reasoning but stay within Grade 8 math capabilities (no trigonometry).
- Heredity (SCI004) connects backward to Grade 7 natural selection and forward to high school biology. Ensure the DNA/protein model is conceptually sound.
- American history coverage (SS004) is extensive. Prioritize depth over breadth; focus on cause-and-effect reasoning and primary source analysis rather than date memorization.
- Common Core standards referenced: 8.NS.A.1-2, 8.EE.A.1-4, 8.EE.B.5-6, 8.EE.C.7-8, 8.F.A.1-3, 8.F.B.4-5, 8.G.A.1-5, 8.G.B.6-8, 8.G.C.9, 8.SP.A.1-4.

- [ ] Systems of equations include all three solution methods (graphing, substitution, elimination)
