**Capstone year.** Mathematics options vary widely across students (Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Statistics, or review). This PRD covers the Pre-Calculus / Calculus path as the most comprehensive option; students not on this track can use subsets. Science is often Environmental Science or an AP elective. ELA focuses on college-ready writing and world/British literature. Social Studies covers Government and Economics in depth.

## Subjects and Units

### Mathematics -- Pre-Calculus / Calculus (Common Core HSF-TF, HSF-BF, HSN-VM, plus calculus concepts beyond Common Core)

**PRECALC001: Advanced Functions**
- Analyze polynomial functions: end behavior, zeros with multiplicity, turning points (HSF-IF.C.7c)
- Analyze rational functions: vertical/horizontal/slant asymptotes, domain restrictions, holes (HSF-IF.C.7d)
- Analyze exponential and logarithmic functions in applied contexts (HSF-IF.C.7e, HSF-LE.A.4)
- Analyze and graph piecewise-defined functions (HSF-IF.C.7b)

**PRECALC002: Trigonometry**
- Prove and apply trigonometric identities: Pythagorean, double-angle, half-angle (HSF-TF.C.8, HSF-TF.C.9)
- Use inverse trigonometric functions (HSF-TF.B.6, HSF-TF.B.7)
- Apply the law of sines and law of cosines to solve problems (HSF-TF.C.9)
- Represent and operate with complex numbers in polar form (HSN-CN.B.4, HSN-CN.B.5)
- Graph and analyze parametric equations (extension)
- Convert between rectangular and polar coordinates (extension)

**PRECALC003: Sequences and Series**
- Define and use arithmetic sequences and series; derive and apply sum formulas (HSF-BF.A.2, HSF-LE.A.2)
- Define and use geometric sequences and series; derive and apply sum formulas including infinite geometric series (HSF-BF.A.2, HSF-LE.A.2)
- Apply the Binomial Theorem (HSA-APR.C.5)
- Understand and apply mathematical induction for proof (extension)

**CALC001: Limits and Continuity**
- Understand the concept of a limit informally and formally (calculus extension)
- Evaluate limits using algebraic techniques: direct substitution, factoring, rationalization (calculus extension)
- Determine continuity of a function at a point and on an interval (calculus extension)
- Apply the Intermediate Value Theorem (calculus extension)

**CALC002: Derivatives**
- Understand the derivative as a limit of the difference quotient (calculus extension)
- Apply differentiation rules: power rule, product rule, quotient rule, chain rule (calculus extension)
- Use implicit differentiation (calculus extension)
- Solve related rates problems (calculus extension)
- Solve optimization problems using derivatives (calculus extension)

**CALC003: Integrals**
- Understand antiderivatives and indefinite integrals (calculus extension)
- Evaluate definite integrals using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (calculus extension)
- Apply basic integration techniques: substitution, integration by parts (calculus extension)
- Calculate the area between curves (calculus extension)

**SP006: Advanced Statistics (Optional Track)**
- Understand and apply the normal distribution (HSS-ID.A.4)
- Construct and interpret confidence intervals (HSS-IC.B.4)
- Conduct and interpret hypothesis tests (HSS-IC.B.5)
- Apply chi-square tests for goodness of fit and independence (extension)
- Perform and interpret regression analysis: linear and nonlinear (HSS-ID.B.6, HSS-ID.C.8)

### ELA -- English IV / British Literature / World Literature (Common Core RL.11-12, RI.11-12, W.11-12, SL.11-12, L.11-12)

**LA001: Reading Literature**
- Analyze world and British literature across historical periods and cultural contexts (RL.11-12.6, RL.11-12.7)
- Compare and contrast treatments of themes across cultures and periods (RL.11-12.9)
- Evaluate literary criticism and apply critical lenses to interpretation (RL.11-12.7)
- Conduct independent literary analysis with sustained, text-grounded argument (RL.11-12.1)

**LA002: Reading Informational Text**
- Evaluate complex arguments across genres: essays, speeches, journalism, policy documents (RI.11-12.8)
- Analyze rhetoric across genres and evaluate its effectiveness (RI.11-12.6)
- Synthesize information from multiple complex sources to address a question or solve a problem (RI.11-12.7)
- Apply media literacy skills: evaluate credibility, bias, and purpose across media types (RI.11-12.7)

**LA003: Writing**
- Write college-ready argumentative essays with sophisticated reasoning and evidence (W.11-12.1)
- Conduct and write extended research papers with proper attribution and synthesis (W.11-12.7, W.11-12.8)
- Write creative works demonstrating command of narrative techniques (W.11-12.3)
- Write personal statements and application essays (extension)

**LA004: Speaking and Listening**
- Deliver formal presentations with clear structure, evidence, and audience awareness (SL.11-12.4)
- Participate in formal debate with structured argumentation (SL.11-12.1, SL.11-12.3)
- Engage in collaborative academic discourse with synthesis of multiple perspectives (SL.11-12.1)

**LA005: Language**
- Demonstrate command of professional register across writing contexts (L.11-12.1, L.11-12.3)
- Apply discipline-specific writing conventions (L.11-12.2)
- Adapt style and tone for audience and purpose (L.11-12.3)

**LA006: Vocabulary**
- Master technical and discipline-specific vocabulary for college readiness (L.11-12.6)
- Use etymology to determine meanings of unknown words (L.11-12.4b)
- Demonstrate precision in word choice for academic and professional writing (L.11-12.5)

### Science -- Environmental Science / AP Science / Elective (NGSS HS-LS2, HS-ESS2, HS-ESS3, HS-ETS1)

**SCI001: Environmental Systems**
- Develop a model to illustrate the role of various processes in ecosystem dynamics at global scale (HS-LS2-4, HS-ESS2-6)
- Use mathematical representations to describe biogeochemical cycles (HS-ESS2-6)
- Model energy flow through ecosystems at planetary scale (HS-LS2-4)

**SCI002: Human Impact**
- Evaluate evidence for human population dynamics and carrying capacity (HS-ESS3-3)
- Analyze data on resource management and depletion (HS-ESS3-1)
- Evaluate claims, evidence, and reasoning about pollution and environmental degradation (HS-ESS3-4)
- Analyze evidence for climate change: causes, effects, projections (HS-ESS3-5)

**SCI003: Sustainability**
- Evaluate design solutions for developing and using renewable energy (HS-ETS1-3)
- Create a computational simulation to illustrate the relationships among management of natural resources, sustainability, and biodiversity (HS-ESS3-3)
- Analyze the intersection of science and policy in environmental decision-making (HS-ESS3-4)

**SCI004: Research Methods**
- Design and conduct scientific investigations with proper controls and variables (HS-ETS1-2)
- Analyze and interpret experimental data using statistical methods (HS-ETS1-4)
- Communicate scientific findings through formal scientific writing (extension)
- Evaluate the validity of scientific claims based on methodology (HS-ETS1-3)

### Social Studies -- Government / Economics (C3 Framework: D2.Civ, D2.Eco, D2.Geo, D2.His)

**SS001: Government**
- Analyze the structure and function of US government in depth: federalism, separation of powers, checks and balances (D2.Civ.3.9-12, D2.Civ.4.9-12)
- Compare US government with other governmental systems (D2.Civ.1.9-12)
- Analyze political ideologies and their influence on policy (D2.Civ.2.9-12)
- Conduct policy analysis: identify problems, evaluate options, assess trade-offs (D2.Civ.13.9-12)

**SS002: Economics**
- Synthesize microeconomic and macroeconomic concepts (D2.Eco.1.9-12, D2.Eco.9.9-12)
- Build and evaluate economic models (D2.Eco.4.9-12)
- Analyze current events through an economic lens (D2.Eco.15.9-12)
- Apply personal finance concepts: budgeting, credit, investment, taxation (D2.Eco.11.9-12)

**SS003: Geography**
- Analyze global issues through a geographic lens (D2.Geo.3.9-12)
- Evaluate international relations and geopolitical dynamics (D2.Geo.8.9-12)
- Analyze environmental geopolitics: resources, climate, and conflict (D2.Geo.5.9-12)

**SS004: History**
- Analyze the contemporary world: post-Cold War era and globalization (D2.His.1.9-12)
- Evaluate 21st century challenges: terrorism, technology, inequality (D2.His.16.9-12)
- Apply historical thinking skills to contemporary issues: sourcing, contextualization, argumentation (D2.His.16.9-12)

## Difficulty Calibration

- Starting difficulty: 5-7
- Ending difficulty: 7-10
- Target range for most content: 6-9
- Calculus content (CALC001-CALC003) represents the highest difficulty in the K-12 sequence. Optimization and integration items should reach difficulty 10. Pre-Calculus content should have a floor of difficulty 5.

## Cross-Grade Prerequisites

### From Grade 11

- `g11-math-FUNC101` (Advanced Functions: polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic) feeds into `g12-math-PRECALC001` (Advanced Function Analysis: deeper treatment with applications)
- `g11-math-FUNC102` (Trigonometric Functions, Unit Circle, Identities) feeds into `g12-math-PRECALC002` (Advanced Trigonometry: inverse functions, law of sines/cosines, polar)
- `g11-math-ALG103` (Polynomial Theorems) feeds into `g12-math-PRECALC001` (Polynomial End Behavior, Rational Function Analysis)
- `g11-math-ALG101` (Rational Exponents) feeds into `g12-math-PRECALC001` (Exponential/Logarithmic Applications)
- `g11-math-SP005` (Making Inferences, Probability) feeds into `g12-math-SP006` (Advanced Statistics: confidence intervals, hypothesis testing)
- `g11-sci-PHYS001`-`PHYS004` (Physics) provide scientific reasoning and mathematical modeling foundation for `g12-sci-SCI001`-`SCI004` (Environmental Science, Research Methods)
- `g11-sci-PHYS002` (Energy, Conservation) feeds into `g12-sci-SCI001` (Environmental Systems, Energy Flow)
- `g11-ela-LA001`-`LA006` (American Literature, Rhetoric) continue into `g12-ela-LA001`-`LA006` (World/British Literature, College-Ready Writing)
- `g11-ss-SS002` (Macroeconomics) feeds into `g12-ss-SS002` (Micro and Macro Synthesis, Economic Modeling)
- `g11-ss-SS004` (Modern US History) feeds into `g12-ss-SS004` (Contemporary World, Globalization)

### To Post-Secondary

- Terminal grade. No K-12 successor grades.
- `g12-math-CALC001`-`CALC003` align with college Calculus I placement.
- `g12-math-SP006` aligns with college introductory Statistics placement.
- `g12-ela-LA003` (College-Ready Writing) targets college composition readiness.
- `g12-ss-SS001` (Government) and `g12-ss-SS002` (Economics) align with college introductory courses.
- Focus on mastery confirmation and readiness benchmarks rather than forward prerequisite chains.

## Graph Requirements

- Target edge density: 3.0-4.0 prerequisite edges per skill node
- Key prerequisite chains:
  - Advanced functions (PRECALC001) -> Limits (CALC001) -> Derivatives (CALC002) -> Integrals (CALC003) (calculus progression -- strictly sequential)
  - Trigonometric identities (PRECALC002) -> Inverse trig (PRECALC002) -> Implicit differentiation (CALC002) -> Integration techniques (CALC003)
  - Sequences and series (PRECALC003) -> Binomial theorem -> Mathematical induction (proof chain)
  - Environmental systems (SCI001) -> Human impact (SCI002) -> Sustainability (SCI003) -> Research methods (SCI004) (environmental science chain)
  - Normal distribution (SP006) -> Confidence intervals -> Hypothesis testing -> Chi-square (statistics chain)
  - World literature analysis (LA001) -> Argument evaluation (LA002) -> Research writing (LA003) (ELA chain)
  - Government structure (SS001) -> Policy analysis -> Economic modeling (SS002) (social studies chain)
- Cross-subject edges: calculus concepts support physics review and environmental modeling, statistical methods support research design in science, economic analysis uses mathematical modeling

## Authoring Notes

- Grade 12 mathematics has the widest variation in student placement of any grade. The Pre-Calculus/Calculus track defined here is the most rigorous path. Consider marking CALC001-CALC003 as optional/advanced so that students on a Pre-Calculus-only or Statistics track have a clear subset to follow.
- SP006 (Advanced Statistics) is marked as an optional track. It can serve as the primary math course for students not on the Calculus path.
- Calculus content (CALC001-CALC003) extends beyond Common Core standards, which do not cover calculus. These units align with standard AP Calculus AB first-semester content. Mark these clearly as extension content.
- Environmental Science is structured as an integrative capstone science course. It should draw on concepts from all prior science years (Biology in Grade 9, Chemistry in Grade 10, Physics in Grade 11) and emphasize systems thinking.
- Research Methods (SCI004) is designed as a capstone science skill. Items should require students to design experiments, analyze data statistically, and communicate findings -- not just recall steps of the scientific method.
- College-ready writing (LA003) should include explicit attention to the expectations of college composition courses: thesis-driven argument, evidence integration, proper citation, and formal register.
- Personal statement and application essay writing (LA003) is an extension skill unique to Grade 12 that addresses a practical student need.
- Government (SS001) should go beyond structural description to include analysis of how government functions in practice, including current events application.
- Personal finance (SS002) is included as an applied economics skill that addresses a widely recognized gap in K-12 education.
- Common Core standards referenced: HSF-IF.C.7, HSF-LE.A.4, HSF-TF.B.6-7, HSF-TF.C.8-9, HSF-BF.A.2, HSN-CN.B.4-5, HSA-APR.C.5, HSS-ID.A.4, HSS-ID.B.6, HSS-ID.C.8, HSS-IC.B.4-5. Calculus units reference AP Calculus AB content standards.
