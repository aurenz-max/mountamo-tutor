## Current State

No content has been authored for Grade 6. No CSV files, no subskills, no prerequisite graph.

**Important context:** Grade 6 marks the start of middle school. Mathematics transitions from arithmetic to algebraic thinking with ratios, expressions, and equations. ELA focuses on argument and analytical writing. Science introduces disciplinary core models at the middle school level. The conceptual leap from Grade 5 to Grade 6 is one of the largest in K-12 education.

- Science: `SCI001` through `SCI004`

Subskill IDs follow the pattern: `G6-{UNIT_ID}-{SKILL_ID}-{SUBSKILL_NUM}` (e.g., `G6-RP001-unit-rates-01`).

Note: RP001 (Ratios and Proportional Relationships), NS001 (The Number System), EE001 (Expressions and Equations), and SP001 (Statistics and Probability) are new domain codes introduced in Grade 6, reflecting the shift to middle school mathematics.

## Cross-Grade Prerequisites

### From Grade 5

- **OPS005** (expressions with parentheses, order of operations) feeds into **EE001** (write/evaluate expressions with exponents, equivalent expressions, solve equations) -- order of operations and expression fluency are prerequisites for algebraic reasoning
- **NBT005** (decimal operations, multi-digit multiplication/division) feeds into **NS001** (multi-digit decimal operations, GCF/LCM) and **RP001** (unit rates require decimal division, percent requires decimal conversion)
- **NF003** (fraction operations, fractions as division) feeds into **NS001** (divide fractions by fractions) and **RP001** (ratios build on fraction understanding)
- **MEAS005** (volume of rectangular prisms, unit conversions) feeds into **GEOM006** (volume with fractional edge lengths, surface area)
- **GEOM005** (coordinate plane first quadrant, shape classification) feeds into **GEOM006** (coordinate geometry, polygons in coordinate plane) and **NS001** (coordinate plane with all four quadrants, plotting negative numbers)
- **LA001** and **LA002** (Grade 5 literary analysis, integrate multiple sources) feed into **LA001** and **LA002** (cite textual evidence, evaluate arguments, analyze author's purpose)

### To Grade 7

- **RP001** (ratios, unit rates, percent) feeds into Grade 7 proportional relationships (proportional equations, constant of proportionality, percent applications)
- **NS001** (rational number operations, negative numbers) feeds into Grade 7 rational number arithmetic (operations with negatives, rational number properties)
- **EE001** (one-step equations, equivalent expressions) feeds into Grade 7 multi-step equations and inequalities
- **SP001** (statistics: center, spread, distributions) feeds into Grade 7 probability and comparative statistics
- **GEOM006** (area, volume, surface area) feeds into Grade 7 scale drawings, cross-sections, and angle relationships

## Authoring Notes

1. **Lumina-first design (CRITICAL).** Every subskill must target a named Lumina primitive, problem type, or AI tutor session. See parent PRD §5. Starting at Grade 6, ELA shifts toward analytical skills (literary analysis, argumentative writing, rhetoric). These are highly renderable in Lumina when framed correctly:

   **Key ELA primitive targets for Grade 6:**
   - LA001 (Reading Literature): `story-map`, `character-web`, `evidence-finder`, `figurative-language-finder`, `genre-explorer`, `poetry-lab`
   - LA002 (Reading Informational): `text-structure-analyzer`, `evidence-finder`, `timeline-explorer`
   - LA003 (Writing): `paragraph-architect`, `opinion-builder`, `revision-workshop`, AI tutor sessions for drafting feedback
   - LA004 (Speaking and Listening): **Restructure as "Listening Comprehension and Oral Analysis"** — use `listen-and-respond`, `media-player` (for analyzing presentations/media), AI tutor sessions
   - LA005 (Language): `sentence-builder`, `sentence-analyzer`, `fill-in-blanks`
   - LA006 (Vocabulary): `context-clues-detective`, `word-builder`, `figurative-language-finder`, `vocabulary-explorer`

   **Science:** Middle school science is well-served by existing primitives (`atom-builder`, `cell-builder`, `food-web-builder`, `life-cycle-sequencer`, `day-night-seasons`, etc.). Map each subskill to specific science primitives.

2. **Ratios as a new way of thinking:** RP001 introduces proportional reasoning, which is fundamentally different from additive reasoning that dominated K-5. Students must shift from "how much more?" to "how many times as much?" This conceptual shift should be explicitly scaffolded with multiple representations: tape diagrams, double number lines, ratio tables, and coordinate graphs.

2. **Negative numbers:** NS001 introduces negative numbers for the first time. Subskills should progress from real-world contexts (temperature, elevation, debt) to number line placement to operations. Absolute value should be connected to distance, not presented as an isolated rule.

3. **Algebraic expressions and equations:** EE001 bridges arithmetic and algebra. Students write expressions with variables, generate equivalent expressions using properties, and solve one-step equations. The emphasis should be on understanding what a variable represents and what it means to solve an equation (finding a value that makes a statement true), not just mechanical manipulation.

4. **Statistics as a discipline:** SP001 introduces statistics as a way of reasoning about data variability. Students should understand why we need measures of center and spread (because data varies), not just how to compute mean/median/mode. Data displays (dot plots, histograms, box plots) should be taught as tools for answering statistical questions, not as isolated procedures.

5. **Middle school science transition:** Science units now align to NGSS Middle School standards (MS-PS1, MS-LS1, MS-ESS1, MS-ETS1). The level of abstraction increases significantly -- students work with atomic models, cell theory, and plate tectonic theory. Subskills should scaffold from observable phenomena to underlying models.

6. **Ancient civilizations depth:** SS004 covers four major ancient civilizations. Each should have sufficient subskill depth to cover geography, government, economy, culture, and lasting contributions. Comparative analysis subskills should explicitly connect civilizations rather than treating them as isolated units.

7. **Standards alignment:** Mathematics aligns to Common Core Grade 6 (entirely new domains: 6.RP, 6.NS, 6.EE, 6.SP). Science aligns to NGSS Middle School standards. ELA aligns to Common Core Grade 6. Note that Arts is no longer included as a core subject at the middle school level.
