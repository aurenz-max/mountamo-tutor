**Geometry year.** Mathematics is typically Geometry, which shifts emphasis from algebraic manipulation to deductive reasoning and proof. Science is often Chemistry, introducing quantitative problem-solving at the molecular level. ELA deepens literary analysis with attention to seminal US documents.

## Subjects and Units

### Mathematics -- Geometry (Common Core HSG-CO, HSG-SRT, HSG-C, HSG-GPE, HSG-GMD, HSG-MG)

**GEOM101: Congruence**
- Know precise definitions of angle, circle, perpendicular/parallel lines, line segment (HSG-CO.A.1)
- Represent transformations in the plane (HSG-CO.A.2, HSG-CO.A.3, HSG-CO.A.4, HSG-CO.A.5)
- Use geometric descriptions of rigid motions to transform figures and predict effects (HSG-CO.B.6)
- Use the definition of congruence in terms of rigid motions (HSG-CO.B.7, HSG-CO.B.8)
- Prove triangle congruence theorems: SAS, ASA, SSS, AAS (HSG-CO.B.8)
- Prove theorems about lines, angles, triangles, and parallelograms (HSG-CO.C.9, HSG-CO.C.10, HSG-CO.C.11)

**GEOM102: Similarity**
- Verify properties of dilations (HSG-SRT.A.1)
- Use the definition of similarity in terms of similarity transformations (HSG-SRT.A.2)
- Prove similarity theorems: AA, SAS, SSS (HSG-SRT.A.3)
- Prove theorems about triangles using similarity (HSG-SRT.B.4, HSG-SRT.B.5)
- Define trigonometric ratios and solve problems involving right triangles (HSG-SRT.C.6, HSG-SRT.C.7, HSG-SRT.C.8)

**GEOM103: Circles**
- Identify and describe relationships among central angles, inscribed angles, radii, and chords (HSG-C.A.1, HSG-C.A.2, HSG-C.A.3)
- Construct tangent lines to a circle (HSG-C.A.4)
- Derive the formula for arc length and sector area using similarity (HSG-C.B.5)

**GEOM104: Coordinate Geometry**
- Use coordinates to prove simple geometric theorems algebraically (HSG-GPE.B.4)
- Prove the slope criteria for parallel and perpendicular lines (HSG-GPE.B.5)
- Find the point that partitions a segment in a given ratio (HSG-GPE.B.6)
- Use coordinates to compute perimeters and areas of polygons (HSG-GPE.B.7)
- Derive the equation of a circle given center and radius (HSG-GPE.A.1)
- Use distance and midpoint formulas (HSG-GPE.B.4)

**GEOM105: Geometric Measurement**
- Give an informal argument for the formulas for circumference, area, and volume (HSG-GMD.A.1)
- Use Cavalieri's principle to derive volume formulas (HSG-GMD.A.2)
- Use volume formulas for prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones, and spheres (HSG-GMD.A.3)
- Identify the shapes of two-dimensional cross-sections of three-dimensional objects (HSG-GMD.B.4)

**GEOM106: Modeling with Geometry**
- Use geometric shapes, measures, and properties to describe objects (HSG-MG.A.1)
- Apply concepts of density based on area and volume (HSG-MG.A.2)
- Apply geometric methods to solve design problems (HSG-MG.A.3)

### ELA -- English II (Common Core RL.9-10, RI.9-10, W.9-10, SL.9-10, L.9-10 -- deeper application)

**LA001: Reading Literature**
- Analyze the impact of the author's choices regarding how to develop and relate elements of a story (RL.9-10.3, RL.9-10.5)
- Analyze a particular point of view or cultural experience reflected in a work of literature (RL.9-10.6)
- Analyze how an author draws on and transforms source material in a specific work (RL.9-10.9)
- Evaluate multiple interpretations of a work of literature (RL.9-10.7)

**LA002: Reading Informational Text**
- Analyze seminal US documents of historical and literary significance: Declaration of Independence, Gettysburg Address, Letter from Birmingham Jail (RI.9-10.9)
- Analyze in detail how an author's ideas are developed and refined (RI.9-10.5)
- Evaluate the effectiveness of the structure an author uses in an argument (RI.9-10.5, RI.9-10.8)
- Determine the author's purpose and analyze how rhetoric advances that purpose (RI.9-10.6)

**LA003: Writing**
- Write arguments, informative, and narrative texts with increasing sophistication (W.9-10.1, W.9-10.2, W.9-10.3)
- Emphasis on research synthesis and development of personal style (W.9-10.7, W.9-10.8)
- Use technology to produce, publish, and update writing products (W.9-10.6)

**LA004: Speaking and Listening**
- Evaluate a speaker's point of view, reasoning, and use of evidence and rhetoric in formal and informal settings (SL.9-10.3)
- Make strategic use of digital media in presentations (SL.9-10.5)

**LA005: Language**
- Use various reference materials to resolve usage issues (L.9-10.1)
- Apply knowledge of language to understand how syntax contributes to meaning and effect (L.9-10.3)

**LA006: Vocabulary**
- Interpret figurative language in context (L.9-10.5a)
- Analyze the cumulative impact of word choice on meaning and tone (L.9-10.4, RI.9-10.4)
- Acquire domain-specific vocabulary across disciplines (L.9-10.6)

### Science -- Chemistry (NGSS HS-PS1, HS-PS3)

**CHEM001: Atomic Structure**
- Use the periodic table as a model to predict relative properties of elements (HS-PS1-1)
- Construct and revise explanation of electron configuration and atomic models (HS-PS1-1)
- Analyze periodic trends: electronegativity, ionization energy, atomic radius (HS-PS1-2)
- Understand isotopes and atomic mass calculations (HS-PS1-1)

**CHEM002: Chemical Bonding**
- Construct and revise an explanation for ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding (HS-PS1-2)
- Draw Lewis structures for molecules and polyatomic ions (HS-PS1-2)
- Use VSEPR theory to predict molecular geometry (HS-PS1-2)
- Explain polarity of molecules and intermolecular forces (HS-PS1-3)

**CHEM003: Chemical Reactions**
- Balance chemical equations and classify reaction types (HS-PS1-7)
- Use stoichiometry to predict quantities in reactions (HS-PS1-7)
- Determine limiting reactant and calculate percent yield (HS-PS1-7)
- Design solutions by applying scientific principles of chemical reactions (HS-PS1-6)

**CHEM004: Thermochemistry**
- Plan and conduct an investigation to measure energy changes in reactions (HS-PS3-4)
- Apply concepts of enthalpy and Hess's law (HS-PS3-1)
- Use calorimetry to determine energy changes (HS-PS3-4)
- Analyze the role of energy in chemical reaction feasibility (HS-PS3-1)

**CHEM005: States of Matter**
- Use mathematical representations of the ideal gas law (HS-PS1-8)
- Explain phase changes using kinetic molecular theory (HS-PS1-4)
- Describe solution formation and calculate concentration (molarity) (HS-PS1-5)
- Analyze factors affecting solubility and rate of dissolution (HS-PS1-5)

### Social Studies -- US History (C3 Framework: D2.Civ, D2.Eco, D2.Geo, D2.His)

**SS001: Civics**
- Analyze Constitutional development and the amendment process (D2.Civ.3.9-12)
- Evaluate landmark civil rights legislation and its ongoing impact (D2.Civ.10.9-12)

**SS002: Economics**
- Analyze American economic development from agrarian to industrial economy (D2.Eco.1.9-12)

- Analyze the economic policies and impacts of the New Deal (D2.Eco.10.9-12)

**SS003: Geography**
- Analyze the geographic factors in US expansion and manifest destiny (D2.Geo.3.9-12)
- Evaluate patterns and consequences of urbanization (D2.Geo.7.9-12)
- Analyze human-environment interaction in American economic development (D2.Geo.4.9-12)

**SS004: History**
- Analyze US history from Reconstruction through World War I (D2.His.1.9-12)
- Evaluate the Progressive Era and reform movements (D2.His.2.9-12)
- Analyze American imperialism: causes, events, consequences (D2.His.14.9-12)

## Difficulty Calibration

- Starting difficulty: 4-6
- Ending difficulty: 7-10
- Target range for most content: 5-8
- Geometry proofs and Chemistry stoichiometry represent the highest difficulty ceiling in Grade 10. Proof-writing items should reach difficulty 9-10.

## Authoring Notes

- Geometry is the only high school math course where deductive proof is a primary skill. Proof items must be carefully scaffolded: start with fill-in-the-blank proofs, progress to guided proofs, then to independent proof construction.
- Trigonometric ratios in Geometry (GEOM102) are limited to right triangles. The unit circle and general trigonometric functions belong to Algebra II (Grade 11). Do not introduce radian measure here.
- Chemistry stoichiometry (CHEM003) is typically the highest-difficulty content in Grade 10 science. Ensure extensive scaffolding from conceptual mole relationships through limiting reactant problems.
- The ELA seminal documents unit (LA002) is explicitly named in Common Core. Items should require analysis of rhetoric and argument structure, not just comprehension.
- US History coverage at Grade 10 (Reconstruction through WWI) overlaps with some Grade 8 content. Grade 10 treatment should require deeper analytical and evaluative thinking, not repetition of the same content at the same level.
- Common Core standards referenced: HSG-CO.A.1-5, HSG-CO.B.6-8, HSG-CO.C.9-11, HSG-SRT.A.1-3, HSG-SRT.B.4-5, HSG-SRT.C.6-8, HSG-C.A.1-4, HSG-C.B.5, HSG-GPE.A.1, HSG-GPE.B.4-7, HSG-GMD.A.1-3, HSG-GMD.B.4, HSG-MG.A.1-3, HS-PS1-1 through HS-PS1-8, HS-PS3-1, HS-PS3-4.
