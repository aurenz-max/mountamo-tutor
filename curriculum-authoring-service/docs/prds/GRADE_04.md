## Cross-Grade Prerequisites

### From Grade 3

- **OPS003** (multiplication/division within 100, fluency) feeds into **OPS004** (multi-step word problems, factor pairs, prime/composite) -- fluency with basic facts is essential before multi-step and number theory
- **NF001** (unit fractions, equivalent fractions, comparing fractions) feeds into **NF002** (equivalent fractions, add/subtract with like denominators, multiply fraction by whole number, decimals) -- conceptual understanding of fractions must precede operations
- **NBT003** (fluent add/subtract within 1000, multiply by multiples of 10) feeds into **NBT004** (place value to 1,000,000, multi-digit arithmetic, division with remainders)
- **MEAS003** (area, perimeter, scaled graphs) feeds into **MEAS004** (unit conversions, area/perimeter applications, angle measurement, line plots)
- **GEOM003** (quadrilateral categories, partitioning shapes as fractions) feeds into **GEOM004** (points/lines/rays/angles, classify triangles, symmetry)
- **LA001** and **LA002** (Grade 3 reading with point of view and main idea) feed into **LA001** and **LA002** (Grade 4 theme, summarize, text structure)

### To Grade 5

- **OPS004** (multi-step problems, patterns with rules) feeds into **OPS005** (numerical expressions with parentheses, order of operations, patterns and relationships)
- **NBT004** (multi-digit arithmetic, place value to 1,000,000) feeds into **NBT005** (place value for decimals, multi-digit multiplication, decimal operations)
- **NF002** (fractions with like denominators, multiply fraction by whole number, decimal notation) feeds into **NF003** (add/subtract unlike denominators, multiply fractions, divide unit fractions)
- **MEAS004** (unit conversions, angle measurement) feeds into **MEAS005** (unit conversions within systems, volume, line plots with fractions)
- **GEOM004** (classify shapes, symmetry) feeds into **GEOM005** (coordinate plane, classify 2D figures by properties, shape hierarchy)

## Authoring Notes

1. **Multi-digit arithmetic mastery:** Grade 4 is where the standard algorithms for addition, subtraction, and multiplication are expected to be fluent. NBT004 should have extensive subskills covering each algorithm with increasing digit counts, estimation strategies, and reasonableness checks.

2. **Fraction operations begin:** NF002 introduces fraction arithmetic (with like denominators only) and the connection between fractions and decimals. The progression must carefully scaffold: equivalent fractions first, then addition/subtraction with like denominators, then multiplication of fraction by whole number, and finally decimal notation as a special case of fractions (tenths and hundredths).

3. **Angle measurement:** MEAS004 introduces protractor use, which is a hands-on skill. Subskills should include reading a protractor, drawing angles of a given measure, and the concept that angle measure is additive. Consider how this maps to digital/interactive primitives.

4. **Text structure analysis:** LA002 requires students to identify and use text structures (chronology, comparison, cause/effect, problem/solution). This is a significant step up from Grade 3 and should be heavily scaffolded with graphic organizers and explicit instruction subskills.

5. **Cross-curricular vocabulary:** Grade 4 introduces Greek and Latin roots (LA006), which directly supports science vocabulary. Build explicit cross-subject edges where scientific terms share roots covered in vocabulary instruction.

6. **Standards alignment:** Mathematics aligns to Common Core Grade 4 (NF002 extends the fraction domain from Grade 3). Science aligns to NGSS Grade 4 and 3-5 Engineering Design. ELA aligns to Common Core Grade 4.
