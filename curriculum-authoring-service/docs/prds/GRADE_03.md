**Important context:** Grade 3 is a major transition year. Multiplication and division are introduced as core operations. Reading shifts from "learning to read" to "reading to learn." Many states begin standardized testing at Grade 3, making this a high-stakes benchmark year.

## ID Conventions

All Grade 3 unit IDs use the following prefixes:

- Mathematics: `OPS003`, `NBT003`, `NF001`, `MEAS003`, `GEOM003`
- Language Arts: `LA001` through `LA006`
- Science: `SCI001` through `SCI005`
- Social Studies: `SS001` through `SS004`

Subskill IDs follow the pattern: `G3-{UNIT_ID}-{SKILL_ID}-{SUBSKILL_NUM}` (e.g., `G3-OPS003-mult-within-100-01`).

Note: NF001 (Fractions) is a new domain introduced in Grade 3. The numbering starts at 001 because no prior grade has a fractions unit.

## Cross-Grade Prerequisites

### From Grade 2

- **OPS002** (addition/subtraction within 100, equal groups concept) feeds into **OPS003** (multiplication/division within 100) -- equal groups, arrays, and repeated addition are the conceptual foundation for multiplication
- **NBT002** (place value to 1000, add/subtract within 1000) feeds into **NBT003** (rounding, fluent add/subtract within 1000, multiply by multiples of 10)
- **GEOM002** (partitioning shapes into halves, thirds, fourths) feeds into **NF001** (unit fractions, fractions on number line) -- partitioning is the gateway to fraction understanding
- **LA006** and **LA007** (Grade 2 Reading Literature and Informational Text) feed into **LA001** and **LA002** -- comprehension strategies must be established before the "reading to learn" transition
- **MEAS002** (Grade 2 measurement with standard units, time to 5 min) feeds into **MEAS003** (time to nearest minute, elapsed time, liquid volume, mass)

### To Grade 4

- **OPS003** (multiplication/division within 100, fluency) feeds into **OPS004** (multi-step word problems, factor pairs, prime/composite numbers)
- **NF001** (unit fractions, equivalent fractions, comparing fractions) feeds into **NF002** (equivalent fractions, add/subtract with like denominators, multiply fraction by whole number, decimals)
- **NBT003** (fluent add/subtract within 1000, multiply by multiples of 10) feeds into **NBT004** (place value to 1,000,000, multi-digit arithmetic, division with remainders)
- **MEAS003** (area, perimeter, scaled graphs) feeds into **MEAS004** (unit conversions, angle measurement, line plots)
- **LA001** and **LA002** (reading literature and informational text with point of view) feed into Grade 4 **LA001** and **LA002** (theme, structural elements, text structure analysis)

## Authoring Notes

1. **Lumina-first design (CRITICAL).** Every subskill must target a named Lumina primitive, problem type, or AI tutor session. See parent PRD §5 (Lumina-First Design Principle). Grade 3 is the "reading to learn" transition — LA subskills must describe what students DO in Lumina, not what teachers facilitate in classrooms.

   **Key LA primitive targets for Grade 3:**
   - LA001 (Reading Literature): `story-map`, `character-web`, `evidence-finder`, `multiple-choice`, `sequencing-activity`
   - LA002 (Reading Informational): `text-structure-analyzer`, `evidence-finder`, `sequencing-activity`, `categorization-activity`
   - LA003 (Writing): `paragraph-architect`, `opinion-builder`, `story-planner`, `revision-workshop`, AI tutor sessions
   - LA004 (Speaking and Listening): **Restructure as "Listening Comprehension and Oral Language"** — use `listen-and-respond`, AI tutor sessions. Remove peer-dependent and presentation subskills.
   - LA005 (Language): `sentence-builder`, `sentence-analyzer`, `fill-in-blanks`, `categorization-activity`
   - LA006 (Vocabulary): `context-clues-detective`, `word-builder`, `figurative-language-finder`, `vocabulary-explorer`

2. **Multiplication and division as the centerpiece:** Grade 3 math revolves around building multiplicative reasoning. OPS003 should be the largest math unit with the most granular subskill breakdown. Cover all interpretations: equal groups, arrays, area model, comparison. Include fact fluency as a distinct skill cluster.

2. **Fractions introduction:** NF001 is new territory. The subskill progression must be carefully scaffolded: whole partitioned into equal parts, naming unit fractions, placing fractions on a number line, then equivalence and comparison. Avoid jumping to symbolic manipulation before conceptual understanding is established.

3. **Reading to learn transition:** This is the year where reading becomes a tool for learning other subjects. LA001 and LA002 should emphasize comprehension strategies that transfer to science and social studies: main idea, supporting details, text features, cause and effect.

4. **Standardized testing preparation:** While the curriculum should not teach to the test, the subskill granularity should ensure coverage of all tested standards. Ensure word problem subskills span all four operations and include multi-step problems.

5. **Area and perimeter distinction:** A common misconception at this level is confusing area and perimeter. MEAS003 should include explicit subskills for distinguishing between the two concepts, not just computing each independently.

6. **Standards alignment:** Mathematics aligns to Common Core Grade 3 (including the new 3.NF domain). Science aligns to NGSS Grade 3 and 3-5 Engineering Design. ELA aligns to Common Core Grade 3 (note the shift from RF to RL/RI emphasis).
