**High school begins.** Subjects become course-based rather than grade-banded. Mathematics is typically Algebra I. Science may vary by state, but Biology is the most common Grade 9 science course. ELA shifts toward sustained literary analysis and argument writing.

## Subjects and Units

### Mathematics -- Algebra I (Common Core HSA-SSE, HSA-APR, HSA-CED, HSA-REI, HSF-IF, HSF-BF, HSF-LE, HSS-ID)

**ALG001: Seeing Structure in Expressions**
- Interpret expressions that represent a quantity in terms of its context (HSA-SSE.A.1)
- Use the structure of an expression to identify ways to rewrite it (HSA-SSE.A.2)
- Choose and produce an equivalent form of an expression to reveal properties: factor quadratics (HSA-SSE.B.3a)
- Complete the square in a quadratic expression (HSA-SSE.B.3b)

**ALG002: Arithmetic with Polynomials**
- Understand that polynomials form a system analogous to the integers (HSA-APR.A.1)
- Add, subtract, and multiply polynomials (HSA-APR.A.1)
- Know and apply polynomial identities (HSA-APR.C.4)

**ALG003: Creating Equations**
- Create equations and inequalities in one variable and use them to solve problems (HSA-CED.A.1)
- Create equations in two or more variables to represent relationships (HSA-CED.A.2)
- Represent constraints by equations or inequalities and interpret solutions (HSA-CED.A.3)
- Rearrange formulas to highlight a quantity of interest (HSA-CED.A.4)

**ALG004: Reasoning with Equations and Inequalities**
- Explain each step in solving a simple equation (HSA-REI.A.1)
- Solve linear equations and inequalities in one variable (HSA-REI.B.3)
- Solve quadratic equations in one variable (HSA-REI.B.4)
- Solve systems of linear equations exactly and approximately (HSA-REI.C.5, HSA-REI.C.6)
- Represent and solve equations and inequalities graphically (HSA-REI.D.10, HSA-REI.D.11, HSA-REI.D.12)

**FUNC002: Interpreting Functions**
- Understand the concept of a function and use function notation (HSF-IF.A.1, HSF-IF.A.2)
- Recognize the domain of a function (HSF-IF.A.1)
- For a function that models a relationship, interpret key features of graphs and tables (HSF-IF.B.4)
- Calculate and interpret the average rate of change of a function (HSF-IF.B.6)
- Graph functions expressed symbolically: linear, quadratic, absolute value, step, piecewise (HSF-IF.C.7)
- Compare properties of two functions represented in different ways (HSF-IF.C.9)

**FUNC003: Building Functions**
- Write a function that describes a relationship between two quantities (HSF-BF.A.1)
- Write arithmetic and geometric sequences with explicit and recursive formulas (HSF-BF.A.2)
- Identify the effect of transformations on the graph of a function (HSF-BF.B.3)

**FUNC004: Linear, Quadratic, and Exponential Models**
- Distinguish between linear, quadratic, and exponential growth situations (HSF-LE.A.1)
- Construct linear and exponential functions given a graph, description, or input-output pairs (HSF-LE.A.2)
- Observe that exponential functions eventually exceed polynomial functions (HSF-LE.A.3)
- Interpret the parameters in a linear or exponential function in context (HSF-LE.B.5)

**SP004: Interpreting Categorical and Quantitative Data**
- Represent data with plots on the real number line (HSS-ID.A.1)
- Summarize and compare distributions using statistics appropriate to the shape (HSS-ID.A.2, HSS-ID.A.3)
- Summarize categorical data in two-way frequency tables (HSS-ID.B.5)
- Represent data on scatter plots; describe fit using functions (HSS-ID.B.6)
- Interpret slope and intercept of a linear model in context (HSS-ID.C.7)
- Compute and interpret correlation coefficient; distinguish correlation from causation (HSS-ID.C.8, HSS-ID.C.9)

### ELA -- English I (Common Core RL.9-10, RI.9-10, W.9-10, SL.9-10, L.9-10)

**LA001: Reading Literature**
- Cite strong and thorough textual evidence to support analysis (RL.9-10.1)
- Determine a theme or central idea and analyze in detail its development (RL.9-10.2)
- Analyze how complex characters develop, interact, and advance the plot or theme (RL.9-10.3)
- Analyze the representation of a subject or key scene in two different artistic mediums (RL.9-10.7)
- Analyze how an author draws on and transforms source material (RL.9-10.9)
- Analyze cultural experience reflected in world literature (RL.9-10.6)

**LA002: Reading Informational Text**
- Cite strong and thorough textual evidence (RI.9-10.1)
- Determine a central idea and analyze its development with objective summary (RI.9-10.2)
- Analyze how an author unfolds an analysis or series of ideas (RI.9-10.3)
- Determine the meaning of words and phrases including figurative, connotative, and technical meanings (RI.9-10.4)
- Determine an author's point of view or purpose and analyze how rhetoric advances it (RI.9-10.6)
- Delineate and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text (RI.9-10.8)

**LA003: Writing**
- Write arguments to support claims with precise reasoning and relevant evidence; address counterclaims (W.9-10.1)
- Write informative/explanatory texts to examine complex topics (W.9-10.2)
- Write narratives with multiple plot lines and well-structured sequences (W.9-10.3)
- Conduct sustained research projects with synthesis from multiple sources (W.9-10.7, W.9-10.8)

**LA004: Speaking and Listening**
- Initiate and participate in collaborative discussions on complex topics (SL.9-10.1)
- Integrate multiple sources of information presented in diverse formats (SL.9-10.2)
- Evaluate a speaker's point of view, reasoning, and use of evidence (SL.9-10.3)
- Present information clearly, concisely, and logically (SL.9-10.4)

**LA005: Language**
- Use parallel structure (L.9-10.1a)
- Use various types of phrases and clauses to convey specific meanings and add variety (L.9-10.1b)
- Use a semicolon to link independent clauses; use a colon to introduce a list or quotation (L.9-10.2a)
- Spell correctly (L.9-10.2c)

**LA006: Vocabulary**
- Use context as a clue to determine technical and domain-specific meanings (L.9-10.4a)
- Identify and correctly use patterns of word changes (L.9-10.4b)
- Interpret figures of speech: euphemism and oxymoron (L.9-10.5a)
- Analyze nuances in the meaning of words with similar denotations (L.9-10.5b)
- Acquire and use academic and domain-specific vocabulary (L.9-10.6)

### Science -- Biology (NGSS HS-LS1, HS-LS2, HS-LS3, HS-LS4)

**BIO001: Structure and Function**
- Construct an explanation of cell structure and how systems of specialized cells work together (HS-LS1-1, HS-LS1-2)
- Plan and conduct an investigation of the role of feedback mechanisms in maintaining homeostasis (HS-LS1-3)
- Use a model to illustrate how feedback mechanisms maintain homeostasis (HS-LS1-3)

**BIO002: Ecosystems**
- Use mathematical and/or computational representations for the cycling of matter and flow of energy among organisms (HS-LS2-4)
- Evaluate claims about the effect of human activities on biodiversity (HS-LS2-7)
- Develop a model to illustrate energy flow through trophic levels (HS-LS2-4)
- Use models to describe the carbon cycle (HS-LS2-5)
- Evaluate the evidence for the role of group behavior in individual and species survival (HS-LS2-8)

**BIO003: Heredity**
- Ask questions to clarify the mechanisms of DNA replication (HS-LS3-1)
- Construct an explanation of protein synthesis from DNA to protein (HS-LS3-1)
- Apply concepts of Mendelian genetics and probability to predict trait inheritance (HS-LS3-3)
- Make and defend a claim about the ethical implications of genetic engineering (HS-LS3-3)

**BIO004: Evolution**
- Communicate scientific information that common ancestry is supported by multiple lines of evidence (HS-LS4-1)
- Construct an explanation based on evidence for how natural selection leads to adaptation (HS-LS4-4)
- Apply concepts of statistics and probability to explain the mechanisms of evolution: Hardy-Weinberg (HS-LS4-3)
- Evaluate the evidence for speciation (HS-LS4-5)
- Construct and analyze phylogenetic trees (HS-LS4-2)

### Social Studies -- World History / Geography (C3 Framework: D2.Civ, D2.Eco, D2.Geo, D2.His)

**SS001: Civics**
- Analyze foundational political philosophies and Enlightenment thinkers (D2.Civ.1.9-12)
- Compare forms of government and their ideological foundations (D2.Civ.3.9-12)

**SS002: Economics**
- Compare economic systems: capitalism, socialism, communism (D2.Eco.1.9-12)
- Analyze market structures and their effects on price and output (D2.Eco.4.9-12)

**SS003: Geography**
- Analyze world regions in depth using geographic tools and data (D2.Geo.1.9-12)
- Evaluate geopolitical patterns and their geographic underpinnings (D2.Geo.3.9-12)
- Analyze demographic patterns: population pyramids, fertility rates, migration (D2.Geo.7.9-12)

**SS004: History**
- Analyze ancient and classical civilizations through the medieval period with thematic connections (D2.His.1.9-12)
- Evaluate the development and spread of religions and cultural exchange (D2.His.4.9-12)
- Apply historical thinking skills: sourcing, contextualization, corroboration (D2.His.16.9-12)

## Difficulty Calibration

- Starting difficulty: 4-5
- Ending difficulty: 6-10
- Target range for most content: 5-8
- High school content reaches difficulty 10 at the top end for the first time. Algebra I quadratics and Biology evolution/genetics content should have challenging items at this level.

## Graph Requirements

- Target edge density: 3.0-4.0 prerequisite edges per skill node
- Key prerequisite chains:
  - Linear functions (FUNC002) -> Quadratic expressions (ALG001) -> Solving quadratics (ALG004) -> Model comparison (FUNC004) (central algebra chain)
  - Function notation (FUNC002) -> Building functions (FUNC003) -> Transformations -> Sequence formulas
  - Creating equations (ALG003) -> Systems of equations (ALG004) -> Graphical representation
  - Cell structure (BIO001) -> DNA/protein synthesis (BIO003) -> Mendelian genetics -> Evolution (BIO004) (biology chain)
  - Evidence analysis (LA002) -> Argument writing (LA003) -> Research synthesis (ELA chain)
  - Political philosophies (SS001) -> Economic systems (SS002) -> Historical analysis (SS004)
- Cross-subject edges: algebraic modeling in biology (Hardy-Weinberg uses probability), data interpretation in science, evidence-based argument structure shared between ELA and science

## Authoring Notes

- Algebra I is the gateway course for all subsequent high school mathematics. Ensure thorough coverage of all HSA and HSF standards, with particular attention to the conceptual understanding of functions, not just procedural fluency.
- The transition from middle school to high school ELA standards (RL/RI.9-10 vs RL/RI.8) involves a significant jump in text complexity and analytical depth. Include scaffolded items that bridge this gap.
- Biology covers the full HS-LS domain in one year. This is a large scope; ensure units are tightly sequenced and that evolution (BIO004) builds on heredity (BIO003) rather than standing alone.
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (BIO004) requires probability concepts from Algebra I (SP004). Ensure this cross-subject dependency is reflected in the prerequisite graph.
- World History / Geography at Grade 9 varies significantly across states and districts. The units defined here cover common content; districts may reorder or substitute.
- Common Core standards referenced: HSA-SSE.A.1-2, HSA-SSE.B.3, HSA-APR.A.1, HSA-APR.C.4, HSA-CED.A.1-4, HSA-REI.A.1, HSA-REI.B.3-4, HSA-REI.C.5-6, HSA-REI.D.10-12, HSF-IF.A.1-2, HSF-IF.B.4-6, HSF-IF.C.7-9, HSF-BF.A.1-2, HSF-BF.B.3, HSF-LE.A.1-3, HSF-LE.B.5, HSS-ID.A.1-3, HSS-ID.B.5-6, HSS-ID.C.7-9, RL.9-10.1-9, RI.9-10.1-8, W.9-10.1-8, SL.9-10.1-4, L.9-10.1-6.

### Grade 9-Specific Quality Gates
- [ ] Quadratic items include factoring, completing the square, and quadratic formula approaches
- [ ] Function items require interpretation across representations (symbolic, graphical, tabular, verbal)
- [ ] Biology items assess model-based reasoning, not just vocabulary recall
- [ ] Hardy-Weinberg items explicitly connect to probability and statistical concepts
- [ ] ELA items use grade-appropriate text complexity (Lexile 1050-1335 or equivalent)
- [ ] Writing items require counterclaim acknowledgment in argumentative mode
- [ ] World History items require application of historical thinking skills, not just chronological recall
