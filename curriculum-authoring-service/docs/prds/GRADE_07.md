## Subjects and Units

### Mathematics (Common Core 7.RP, 7.NS, 7.EE, 7.G, 7.SP)

**RP002: Ratios and Proportional Relationships**
- Compute unit rates associated with ratios of fractions, including complex fractions (7.RP.A.1)
- Recognize and represent proportional relationships between quantities (7.RP.A.2)
- Use proportional relationships to solve multi-step ratio and percent problems, including scale drawings (7.RP.A.3)
- Percent increase and decrease (7.RP.A.3)
- Percent applications: tax, tip, markup, discount, simple interest (7.RP.A.3)

**NS002: The Number System**
- Add and subtract rational numbers (7.NS.A.1)
- Multiply and divide rational numbers (7.NS.A.2)
- Apply properties of operations to rational number arithmetic (7.NS.A.3)
- Absolute value in context (7.NS.A.1)
- Convert fluently between fractions, decimals, and percents (7.NS.A.2d)

**EE002: Expressions and Equations**
- Apply properties to generate equivalent expressions (7.EE.A.1, 7.EE.A.2)
- Solve multi-step linear equations in one variable (7.EE.B.3, 7.EE.B.4a)
- Solve multi-step linear inequalities in one variable (7.EE.B.4b)
- Model real-world situations with expressions and equations (7.EE.B.3)

**GEOM007: Geometry**
- Solve problems involving scale drawings of geometric figures (7.G.A.1)
- Draw geometric shapes with given conditions; describe cross-sections of 3D figures (7.G.A.2, 7.G.A.3)
- Know the formulas for area and circumference of a circle (7.G.B.4)
- Use supplementary, complementary, and vertical angle relationships (7.G.B.5)
- Apply the triangle inequality theorem (7.G.B.5)

**SP002: Statistics and Probability**
- Understand random sampling and use it to draw inferences about a population (7.SP.A.1, 7.SP.A.2)
- Draw informal comparative inferences about two populations (7.SP.B.3, 7.SP.B.4)
- Develop, use, and evaluate probability models (7.SP.C.5, 7.SP.C.6, 7.SP.C.7)
- Find probabilities of compound events using organized lists, tables, tree diagrams, and simulation (7.SP.C.8)

### ELA (Common Core RL.7, RI.7, W.7, SL.7, L.7)

**LA001: Reading Literature**
- Cite several pieces of textual evidence to support analysis (RL.7.1)
- Determine a theme and analyze its development over the course of the text (RL.7.2)
- Analyze how particular elements of a story interact (character, setting, plot) (RL.7.3)
- Compare and contrast a written story to its audio, filmed, staged, or multimedia version (RL.7.7)

**LA002: Reading Informational Text**
- Cite several pieces of textual evidence to support analysis (RI.7.1)
- Determine two or more central ideas and analyze their development (RI.7.2)
- Analyze the structure an author uses to organize a text (RI.7.5)
- Determine an author's point of view or purpose (RI.7.6)
- Evaluate the soundness of reasoning and the relevance/sufficiency of evidence in arguments (RI.7.8)

**LA003: Writing**
- Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence (W.7.1)
- Write arguments with claims and counterclaims (W.7.1)
- Write informative/explanatory texts (W.7.2)
- Write narratives with well-chosen details and structured event sequences (W.7.3)
- Conduct short and sustained research projects (W.7.7, W.7.8)

**LA004: Speaking and Listening**
- Engage effectively in collaborative discussions (SL.7.1)
- Analyze the main ideas and supporting details in diverse media and formats (SL.7.2)
- Present claims and findings with pertinent descriptions, facts, details, and examples (SL.7.4)

**LA005: Language**
- Explain the function of phrases and clauses in general and their function in specific sentences (L.7.1a)
- Choose among simple, compound, complex, and compound-complex sentences (L.7.1b)
- Recognize and correct dangling and misplaced modifiers (L.7.1c)

**LA006: Vocabulary**
- Determine the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words using context clues (L.7.4a)
- Use Greek and Latin roots and affixes as clues to the meaning of a word (L.7.4b)
- Interpret figures of speech, including allusion and analogy (L.7.5a)
- Distinguish among the connotations of words with similar denotations (L.7.5c)
- Acquire and use domain-specific vocabulary (L.7.6)

### Science (NGSS MS-PS1, MS-LS2, MS-LS4, MS-ESS2, MS-ETS1)

**SCI001: Chemical Reactions**
- Develop models to describe conservation of matter in chemical reactions (MS-PS1-5)
- Undertake design projects to construct devices that minimize or maximize thermal energy transfer (MS-PS1-6)
- Develop models for synthetic materials derived from natural resources (MS-PS1-3)

**SCI002: Ecosystems**
- Analyze and interpret data for resource availability and population effects (MS-LS2-1)
- Construct an explanation for competitive, predatory, and mutualistic relationships (MS-LS2-2)
- Develop a model to describe the cycling of matter and flow of energy in food webs (MS-LS2-3)
- Evaluate competing design solutions for maintaining biodiversity (MS-LS2-5)

**SCI003: Natural Selection and Adaptation**
- Analyze and interpret data from the fossil record (MS-LS4-1)
- Apply scientific ideas to construct an explanation for the mechanism of natural selection (MS-LS4-4)
- Construct an explanation based on evidence for how natural selection leads to adaptation (MS-LS4-6)
- Gather and synthesize information about artificial selection (MS-LS4-5)

**SCI004: Earth Systems**
- Develop a model to describe the cycling of water through Earth's systems (MS-ESS2-4)
- Collect data to provide evidence for how motions and complex interactions determine weather patterns (MS-ESS2-5)
- Develop and use a model to describe how ocean currents affect weather and climate (MS-ESS2-6)
- Explain factors that affect regional climate (MS-ESS2-6)

**SCI005: Engineering Design**
- Define the criteria and constraints of a design problem with sufficient precision (MS-ETS1-1)
- Evaluate competing design solutions using a systematic process (MS-ETS1-2, MS-ETS1-3)

### Social Studies (C3 Framework: D2.Civ, D2.Eco, D2.Geo, D2.His)

**SS001: Civics**

- Media literacy and evaluating sources (D2.Civ.7.6-8)
- Structure and function of the justice system (D2.Civ.10.6-8)
- Global governance and international organizations (D2.Civ.6.6-8)

**SS002: Economics**
- International trade and comparative advantage (D2.Eco.6.6-8)
- Global economics and interdependence (D2.Eco.9.6-8)
- Economic indicators: GDP, inflation, unemployment (D2.Eco.10.6-8)

**SS003: Geography**
- Human-environment interaction (D2.Geo.4.6-8)
- Urbanization patterns and causes (D2.Geo.7.6-8)
- Migration patterns and push/pull factors (D2.Geo.8.6-8)
- Resource management and sustainability (D2.Geo.5.6-8)

**SS004: History**
- Medieval world: feudalism, Byzantine Empire, Islamic Golden Age (D2.His.1.6-8)

- Reformation: causes, key figures, impacts (D2.His.3.6-8)
- Age of Exploration: motivations, encounters, global exchange (D2.His.14.6-8)

  - Ratios (RP002) -> Proportional Relationships -> Scale Drawings -> Geometry applications

  - Medieval world (SS004) -> Renaissance -> Reformation -> Age of Exploration (chronological chain)
- Cross-subject edges where appropriate (e.g., proportional reasoning in science, data analysis in social studies)

## Authoring Notes

- Grade 7 is the first year where students routinely work with negative rational numbers in all four operations; ensure rich coverage of sign rules in context.
- Proportional relationships are the mathematical backbone of Grade 7 and connect to virtually every other subject. Prioritize deep coverage of RP002.
- Statistics and probability shift from descriptive (Grade 6) to inferential thinking; ensure problems require students to make inferences rather than just compute.
- ELA standards at Grade 7 require citing "several pieces" of evidence (not just one), which is a step up from Grade 6. Ensure evaluation criteria reflect this.
- Science units align with NGSS middle school physical science and life science performance expectations. Engineering design (SCI005) should integrate with other science units rather than stand alone.
- Social Studies covers a broad historical sweep (medieval through Age of Exploration). Maintain thematic connections across units rather than treating them as isolated periods.
- Common Core standards referenced: 7.RP.A.1-3, 7.NS.A.1-3, 7.EE.A.1-2, 7.EE.B.3-4, 7.G.A.1-3, 7.G.B.4-6, 7.SP.A.1-2, 7.SP.B.3-4, 7.SP.C.5-8.

### Standard Quality Gates
- [ ] All subskills have at least 3 assessment items at target difficulty
- [ ] Every skill node has at least 2 prerequisite edges (no orphan nodes)
- [ ] Difficulty calibration verified: items span the full 3-9 range with median in 5-7
- [ ] Cross-grade prerequisite edges validated against Grade 6 and Grade 8 graphs
- [ ] All Common Core / NGSS / C3 standards referenced in unit descriptions are covered by at least one subskill
- [ ] Generator schemas produce valid, well-formed content (tested via eval-test)
- [ ] No answer leakage in prompts, labels, or default UI state

### Grade 7-Specific Quality Gates
- [ ] Proportional reasoning (RP002) subskills are tested in both mathematical and real-world contexts
- [ ] Rational number operations cover all sign combinations (positive/positive, positive/negative, negative/negative) for all four operations
- [ ] Probability models include both theoretical and experimental/simulation-based items
- [ ] ELA reading items require citing multiple pieces of evidence, not single-citation responses
- [ ] Science items assess understanding of models and evidence, not just recall of facts
- [ ] Social Studies items include primary source analysis where appropriate for medieval/Renaissance/Exploration content
